Backing up wallet

In the self-custody model, the customer retains full custody (possession) of his assets at all times, using the service provider simply as an interface to conveniently manage his assets. Only the user has access to his funds. This makes it almost impossible for Xcapit, a government or anyone else to prevent you from using your funds exactly as you wish.

Of course, with great power comes great responsibility! Since only you have access to your funds, you need to manage your wallet carefully. This includes backing up your wallet and following best practices for password management.

The user holds the private key associated with his public address. This, in our case, takes the form of a mnemonic phrase composed of 12 randomly generated words.

The backup process consisted of a copy of the recovery phrase or seed phrase. However, this process generated a lot of friction, as users either did not fully understand what the backup consisted of or did not complete the process due to the length and complexity of the process. I lead the redesign of the backup flow and the incorporation of one more option to protect the seed phrase.

THE PROBLEM

Backing up the wallet is one of the most important actions a user must do when creating a self-custodied wallet. How could we simplify the wallet backup process for users to protect their assets?

Receiving queries about how to recover the wallet, coupled with several complaints about forgetting the seed phrase, made us realise that there were incorrect assumptions about the behaviour of our users. It was clear that there were several problems to solve:

01. Confusing seed phrase backup process.
Users were not completing the backup process because they did not understand exactly what they were doing or found it time consuming and confusing.

02. Forgetting the seed phrase.
Users who managed to complete the backup lost or forgot their recovery phrase, making it impossible to import the wallet in case they needed to recover it.

03. Poor reach.
Users were poorly alerted to the importance of both backing up the wallet and the relevance of the seed phrase.

Security first

Our vision for the new recovery flow allowed users to back up the seed phrase in two simple steps while adding an extra layer of security by creating an encrypted wallet backup file in Google Drive.

Simplifying the seed phrase recovery process

We resolved to implement the recovery phrase backup in two simple steps by clarifying what actions the user must perform in each step.

Adding an extra layer of security

A second layer of security was added by the possibility of creating an encrypted file in Google Drive that could be imported when the wallet needs to be recovered. This option, like the backup of the seed phrase, is not mandatory and can be done at any time. However, users are advised that it is important to do so when creating the wallet.

With this new option, users can recover their wallet in case of forgetting or losing the seed phrase.

More import options

This change also has an impact on the recovery of the wallet, as the user is free to choose either method, as long as both backups have been completed.

Follow-up of the completion of the backup process

To promote the importance of backing up the wallet. Informative modals and a non-invasive reminder system have been incorporated to remind users to complete or back up their wallet.

CONCLUSION

The success resulted in an increasing number of users creating backups of their wallets, which not only indicated an awareness of the importance of securing their assets, but also increased levels of engagement. For our users, the main objective of our team was to launch the redesigned backup flow and the new option of backing up to an encrypted file so that users could choose whichever way was easiest and quickest for them, or both. Beyond that, there were higher level objectives for the company: to increase conversion in the use of the Xcapit wallet and to consolidate our fractured security paradigm into a streamlined experience.
