THE PROBLEM

Participation in the purchase of cryptocurrencies through the Xcapit wallet was low, users initiated a purchase transaction but failed to complete it. How could we improve the purchase experience to make it more dynamic and simpler?

We wanted to understand why users when initiating a cryptocurrency purchase transaction abandoned the process halfway through. Through conversations, data and research, it became clear that there were incorrect assumptions and misaligned expectations about user behaviour. For us, there was not just one problem, but many - how users interacted with and perceived the purchase process.

01. Data request by external suppliers.
The procurement process involved integration with suppliers external to Xcapit that required user data to complete transactions. This generated doubts among users as to why personal data was requested if it was a decentralised wallet.

02. Confusing KWC process or request for verification information.
Unnecessary data was requested and not required by the provider for validation and the process was confusing for users.

03. Poor follow-up.
Users wanted to know the progress of their validation process and the status of their cryptocurrency purchases.

Reducing access barriers to financial freedom

We had a message to drive home: Xcapit is a self-custodial, open source, decentralised wallet. Our vision for the new flow of cryptocurrency purchases allowed users to transform fiat money into crypto more easily. The redesign allowed us to scale the wallet and focus on making the purchase experience more user-friendly, reducing unnecessary steps and making the identity validation process required by external providers more transparent.

New purchase homepage

To provide users with quick information about what is happening with their purchases and to keep them informed about the status of their validation process, we introduced a new shopping homepage, where the user can not only visualise the purchases they are making, but also the status of their validation process.

SOLUTIONS:
Validation process status management.
History of purchases made.

Transparency in the request for information

In order to clear up doubts about the information requested by external suppliers, an informative modal was incorporated before starting the purchasing process and emphasis was placed on visually distinguishing the moments in which the user is operating with the Xcapit wallet and with the supplier.

SOLUTIONS:
Informative modal and updated FAQs.
Visual separation for the user to identify who they are trading with.

Simplification of the KYC process

The process was divided into two stages: a data request stage, where only the information required by the supplier is requested, and an identity verification stage, where the user's identity is validated. This separation of tasks gave the user an order to follow and a better understanding. In addition, the possibility of being able to continue with the next stage at a later date without losing the information registered up to that point was incorporated.

SOLUTIONS:
Creation of an intermediate progress screen.
Possibility of continuing the process later.
